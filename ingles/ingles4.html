An amazing camping trip

— “It’s coming from there,” Alberto pointed out, indicating the house.

Everyone looked where the boy was pointing, but they saw nothing. Saúl noticed some shadows moving threateningly over the stone wall and asked:

— “What are those shadows?”

— “They look like ghosts!” Nela said in amazement.

— “They look like giants with very long arms,” Sara whispered.

— “It’s making me very scared…” Nela said, trembling.

The boys and girls hugged each other. They were about to scream when they saw a familiar silhouette. It looked like… the shadow of a dog wagging its tail!

— “It’s Mol!” Alberto exclaimed.

— “Now I know what’s happening,” Nela explained, laughing. “The light at the entrance is on, and the mysterious figures we see on the wall are the shadows of the trees.”

— “And when the wind blows, they look like monsters moving their arms and howling!” Gonzalo admitted, relieved. Sara then spoke up:

— “Shall we play shadow theatre?”

— “Yesss!” the others shouted excitedly. “That will be fun.”

“Now this is an amazing camping trip!” Alberto thought.

Sara Barrio
