The Moon's Garden

In the garden of the Moon,

apple trees and thyme grow high,

frogs are splashing in a lagoon,

nightingales and crickets nearby.

Adriana is a little frog,

the prettiest one around,

she smells like sweet apples

and jasmine flowers on the ground.

Nicanor the nightingale,

the boldest bird of all,

upon a blooming water lily

has set sail with no fear to fall.

Pepillo, the cheerful cricket,

sings and sings under the stars,

gazing sadly at the little bird:

—When do you think you'll return from afar?

Lola Núñez
